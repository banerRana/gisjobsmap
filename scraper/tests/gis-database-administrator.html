Introduction

Prince William County GIS, a well-established GIS program and winners of multiple state and national achievement awards, is searching for a highly technical, innovative, and enthusiastic individual to be our GIS Database Administrator. If you consider yourself a geodatabase subject matter expert who can provide lead GIS database technical support to a team of GIS Developers, GIS Analysts, and application administrators, then this is the right opportunity for you!

As the GIS Database Administrator, you will be primarily responsible for the ongoing management and maintenance of our enterprise geodatabase. You must have advanced working knowledge of database principles to ensure that our databases are optimized for maximum performance and is designed according to best GIS industry standards. Our enterprise geodatabase is integrated with numerous applications so the individual we are looking for should have a holistic approach to their database administration techniques to be able to successfully support an enterprise system.

We reward our employees' hard work with a friendly and supportive work environment that encourages creativity and innovation. We provide training and career development, ample paid time off, and work-life balance. There are extensive opportunities to learn and grow while being a player in this innovative and dedicated program by being involved in cross functional teams, IT modernization initiatives and building business partnerships to solve problems using GIS technology.

Come join our team and be a leader with us!

PREFERRED SKILLS:
Advanced enterprise geodatabase management, maintenance, and administration skills; Managing the technical aspects of GIS database development and its integration with systems and applications; Proficiency in the use of Esri's software and development platform.

General Definition of Work, Typical tasks, Knowledge, Skills and Abilities

GENERAL DEFINITION OF WORK:

Performs complex technical and professional work in programming and analysis tasks by utilizing multiple platforms, languages, and databases; develops and interprets user requirements into logical specifications; research the use of new technologies; applies programming and analysis skills with a detailed knowledge of the application; coordinates system testing and assists users with acceptance testing; develops work plans and organizes projects; evaluates user requests for new or modified programs to determine feasibility, cost and time requirements; assists with the configuration management for software, hardware, and system environment; coordinates multiple user requests; assists in development and installation of new applications or complex enhancements; working with vendors to implement/configure or upgrade off-the-shelf systems or third party technology.

Work is performed under general supervision of the Division Chief or designee.

DISTINGUISHING CHARACTERISTICS OF THE CLASS:

This is advanced systems analysis/programming work to include a focus in Business Applications, Enterprise/Web Application or Geographic Information Systems. It is distinguished from the Systems Developer I by the more advanced level of systems design and programming, and ability to perform work with less guidance.

TYPICAL TASKS:

Serves as programmer for development, modification or enhancement of complex new application systems projects;
Evaluates new or alternative approaches to systems development;
Performs business process/systems reviews with agency staff;
Identifies and documents requirements;
Develops design and technical specifications;
Prepares procedural manuals and documentation for system users;
Trains system users;
Provides technical assistance to user departments;
Plans and conducts acceptance tests for new systems and modifications to existing systems;
Assures quality of system test results;
Monitors program development, detects, analyzes, and solves systems and programming problems;
Responds to emergencies;
Participates in the implementation of systems with users and other agency personnel;
Participates in the upgrade of systems with users and other agency personnel;
Researches and evaluates new/proposed software, methodologies and technology for effectiveness and applicability;
Assists in the development of requests for proposals and participates in the evaluation of responses and selection of vendors;
Writes new and/or modifies existing computer programs;
Tests and debugs computer programs and corrects program logic when necessary;
Assists in development of management reports;

KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES:

Thorough knowledge of the principles of systems design and analysis, modern techniques of systems analysis, and systems development life cycle; thorough knowledge of multi-platform computer languages, programming principles, practices and particular operating characteristics of the hardware on which the language is processing and of the operating requirements of carious computer platforms functioning in centralized, distributed, client server, and stand-alone environments; thorough knowledge of effective project management methods, practices and techniques; thorough knowledge and understanding of electronic document management systems; thorough knowledge and understanding of interactive voice response systems and/or call systems; thorough knowledge and understanding of mobile applications; thorough knowledge and/or ability to develop workflows; ability to communicate effectively both orally and in writing; ability to plan, organize, and conduct system analysis and to evaluate administrative processes and procedures; ability to efficiently monitor and participate in program development and thoroughly document necessary system modifications and enhancements.

Geographic Information Systems (GIS):

Thorough knowledge of the principles, practice and techniques of Geographic Information Systems technologies; some knowledge of principles and techniques for GIS system designs and analysis; thorough knowledge of the geographic-based analysis principles; some experience in developing and maintaining tools and applications specific to GIS technology.

Education and Experience

Requires any combination of education and experience equivalent to an Associate's degree in computer science or related field, and 3-5 years programming/analysis experience; OR possession of a Bachelor's degree in computer science or related field; and 2-3 years programming/analysis experience in a complex information systems environment.

Special Requirements

Must possess valid driver's license;

Background Checks, which may include but are not limited to: Criminal History Check, Credit History Check, Education Verification Check,
License Verification Check, and/or Fingerprinting may be required as posted in the job advertisement.
